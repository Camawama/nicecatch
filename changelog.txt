## [Unreleased]

### Added
- Fish fight tactics ("phases"): a hooked fish now cycles at random through five
  distinct behaviours during the fight, and the reel minigame changes with each one:
  - **Hold** — it sits still: your clean window to reel it straight in.
  - **Sweep** — it thrashes side to side: keep on it and reel; the thrashing fights
    your crank a little and the follow-camera swings with it.
  - **Pull** — it bores straight away and tears off line: you can't out-crank it, so
    hold on, keep some line, and let it wear itself out.
  - **Sound** — it dives for the bottom: pull the rod up (drag the mouse up) to lift it
    before it will come in; cranking alone barely moves a sounding fish.
  - **Charge** — it rushes back at you dumping slack: crank fast to take up the slack,
    or the fish gets to rest and recover its fight.
  - The next tactic is weighted by how worn out the fish is (fresh fish run, sound and
    charge often; tiring fish hold and thrash; a played-out fish stops running so it can
    be landed), and the same tactic rarely repeats twice in a row.
- Fight HUD hint now coaches the response to the fish's current tactic.
- **Gradual reel-in** for a line with no fish on it. Right-click no longer snaps the line
  straight back; instead **hold right-click and reel** (the same reel mode as a fight —
  camera locks to the bobber, slight zoom — but with no tension and no snapping):
  - **Empty line**: reel at full speed; circle the mouse to reel faster, or just hold for a
    steady baseline. Reel it to within a couple of blocks to retrieve it.
  - **Loot item** (the gold "something's on the line" bite): a little slower, but there's no
    fight and it can't slip off once you're reeling — reel it home to collect it.
  - Let go and the reel-in pauses (camera pans back); hold again to resume. So there are now
    three reel modes overall: nothing hooked (fastest), loot item (slower, no snap), and a
    real fish (the full fight).
  - Can be turned off with `line.gradualReelEnabled` for vanilla instant retrieval.
- **Spool line / boat trolling**: as you move away from a cast bobber the reel pays out line,
  and once you reach the end of the spool (`line.spoolLength`, ~30 blocks) the bobber is
  **dragged along behind you** instead of the line retracting when you get too far. Great for
  trolling a lure behind a moving boat. Toggle with `line.spoolDragEnabled`.

### Changed
- Fish can now bite a **moving** bobber: fish lead a drifting bobber's motion and put on
  extra speed to keep pace with it, so a bobber pushed by a current, towed by a boat, or
  being reeled can still be approached and bitten. The bite-arrival range is a touch more
  forgiving so a moving bobber can still be reached at the moment of the bite.
- The bobber now sits in a hooked fish's **mouth**: it is pinned at the snout and slightly
  low, so the hook at the bottom of the bobber sinks into the front of the head and reads
  as a hook set in the mouth rather than a float hovering above the fish.
- The two-state "running / calm" fight was replaced by the five-tactic phase system above.
  Line snap, tension, fatigue, line length, escape grace and landing rules are unchanged.
- `fight.chargeChance` now weights how often the Charge tactic is chosen (previously the
  chance that a run was a charge). `fight.slackTakeFactor` is unused by the phase system
  and kept only for config compatibility.
